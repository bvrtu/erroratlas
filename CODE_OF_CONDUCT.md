# Code of conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in ErrorAtlas a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, constructive, and specific.
- Assume good intent while remaining accountable for impact.
- Welcome questions and different levels of experience.
- Focus technical criticism on the work, never the person.
- Respect privacy and do not share private source code, credentials, or vulnerability details.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized language, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, and sustained disruption are not acceptable.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this code. They may temporarily or permanently restrict participation for inappropriate behavior.

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository owner's GitHub profile. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as possible.

This code is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).
